## Quantitative Assessment and Treatment of Spasticity

*Northwestern University & Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, 2023 – present*

Spasticity is routinely evaluated with subjective clinical scales. I am developing devices that characterize spasticity quantitatively — using controlled tendon indentation and 3D force-vector analysis — to enable faster and more precise assessments than traditional clinical tests. In parallel, I study pharmacological interventions with minimal side effects compared to conventional treatments, including a pilot study tracking spasticity dynamics in hemiparetic stroke survivors following cyproheptadine administration.

## Engagement-Driven Rehabilitation Systems

*KAIST, 2021 – 2023*

Conventional rehabilitation exercises often fail to sustain patient motivation. I developed an immersive virtual-reality hand rehabilitation system built around a gesture-controlled rhythm game with vibrotactile feedback. fNIRS neuroimaging confirmed that the system activates cortical areas associated with motor planning and execution, multisensory integration, and attention. I also conducted a clinical collaboration examining the effects of donepezil on brain activity and motor recovery during cognitive–motor dual-task training in stroke survivors.

## Upper-Limb Rehabilitation Exoskeletons and fNIRS

*DGIST, 2013 – 2021*

During my Ph.D., I designed clinically relevant upper-limb exoskeletons for stroke survivors with high spasticity. A four-bar linkage and switching mechanism allow each part of the upper limb to be trained with a single motor, and a modular design supports targeted or combined training of the hand, elbow, and shoulder. Two of these designs are patented in Korea (hand and shoulder rehabilitation robots). Using fNIRS with healthy participants and stroke survivors, I identified optimal movement speed and timing conditions for robot-assisted therapy, and showed that game-based engagement improves the reproducibility of therapy-induced brain activation.
