I am a postdoctoral researcher at **Northwestern University** and the **Shirley Ryan AbilityLab** in Chicago, working with Dr. William Z. Rymer on spasticity in stroke survivors. My current research develops quantitative devices that make spasticity assessment faster and more precise than conventional clinical tests, and investigates pharmacological treatments for spasticity with fewer side effects than existing options.

My work sits at the intersection of **robotics, neuroscience, and rehabilitation medicine**. During my Ph.D. at DGIST (advised by Dr. Pyung-Hun Chang), I designed upper-limb rehabilitation exoskeletons for stroke survivors — including patented hand and shoulder rehabilitation robots that deliver multiple therapeutic movements with a single motor — and used functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) to optimize robot-assisted therapy based on measured brain activity. As a postdoctoral fellow at KAIST (with Dr. Hyung-Soon Park), I developed immersive VR- and rhythm-game-based hand rehabilitation systems with multi-sensory feedback to keep patients engaged throughout therapy.

I received my B.S. in Robotics Engineering from Ritsumeikan University and my M.S. in Engineering Science and Bioengineering from Osaka University in Japan as a Monbukagakusho (MEXT) scholar, and my Ph.D. in Robotics Engineering from DGIST in South Korea.

My long-term goal is to lead an interdisciplinary research program that pushes the boundaries of rehabilitation science and technology, and to mentor the next generation of researchers in this field.

## Research Interests

- Rehabilitation robotics for the upper limb
- Quantitative assessment and pharmacological treatment of spasticity
- Engagement-driven rehabilitation: virtual reality, serious games, and multi-sensory feedback
- Neuroimaging (fNIRS) and bio-signal processing (EMG/EEG) for rehabilitation
